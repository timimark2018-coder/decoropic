# QC & Consolidation from China: The Project Inspection Checklist

On a project material order, quality control is not a formality at the end — it's the step that decides whether your finishes arrive right or your fit-out slips. Consolidation is the step that decides whether you pay for one shipment or five. Handled together, they are where a China-sourced package earns its cost advantage. Here's the working checklist.

## Why QC comes before payment, not after

The economics are simple: catching a wrong finish or dimension **at the factory** costs a re-make; catching it **at your site** costs the project. That's why experienced buyers hold the balance payment to a QC gate and require inspection reports before goods leave China.

## The inspection checklist

**Before production**
- Approved control samples for every finish (color, tone, sheen, texture).
- A pre-production sample for key casegoods/joinery.
- A written spec matrix each item is checked against.

**In-line (during production)**
- Early-run check on the first batch to catch systemic issues before the whole order is made.
- Verify materials and hardware match the approved sample, not a substitute.

**Pre-shipment (before it leaves the factory)**
- Random-sample inspection against the approved samples and spec.
- Dimensions, finish, function (drawers, hinges, switches), and quantity.
- Packaging and labelling suitable for sea freight and your market.
- **Photo/video report** documenting findings.

**Payment control**
- Release the balance only after the pre-shipment report is accepted.

## Consolidation: one project, one shipment

Most projects draw from several factories. Consolidation brings them into coordinated FCL/LCL loads:

- **Lower freight** per unit versus multiple small shipments.
- **One set of clearance documents** for your broker.
- **One arrival** to schedule your installation crews around.
- **A single packing manifest** you can reconcile against your spec.

Consolidation also creates a natural final-check point: as goods arrive at the consolidation warehouse, quantities and cartons are verified against the manifest before loading.

## Documentation that travels with the goods

- Commercial invoice and detailed packing list.
- Certificate/Declaration of Origin where your market's duty treatment requires it.
- Any product certifications your destination mandates (arranged where required).
- Fumigation/treatment certificates for timber and packaging where applicable.
